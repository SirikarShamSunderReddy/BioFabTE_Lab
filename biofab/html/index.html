About the Lab

Who Are We

At the Biofabrication and Tissue Engineering (BioFabTE) Lab at the Indian Institute of Technology Hyderabad, we are advancing the frontiers of tissue engineering and regenerative medicine through cutting-edge research in 3D bioprinting.

Our work is driven by the goal of creating biologically meaningful tissue-engineered constructs and physiologically relevant in vitro tissue and organ models that closely recapitulate native tissue structure and function.

Our core expertise lies in the design, processing, and characterisation of tissue-specific decellularised extracellular matrix (dECM) materials. By preserving the native biochemical composition and microenvironmental cues, we develop dECM-based bioinks that actively guide cell behaviour, promote tissue-specific differentiation, and enhance biological functionality in bioprinted constructs.

By integrating principles from biomaterials science, additive manufacturing, and regenerative medicine, the lab seeks to bridge the gap between fundamental biomaterial research and translational biofabrication — addressing key challenges including structural fidelity, biological performance, and scalability.
